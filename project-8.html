Short Circuit

April 2025

Project Overview

Short Circuit is a work which can be interpreted in different ways. Is it a simple representation of self-harm? A critique on how we assign emotion to artificially created objects? I like to think of it as a way to visualize how enabling unhealthy habits can lead to tragic outcomes, but feel free to ponder any other angles.

Circuit consists of a custom-built robot with a, literal, single-track mind. With its copper hands pointing outwards, it is constantly trying to roll forwards towards an electrical outlet. However, no matter how long or hard he tries, a piece of string attached to his back keeps him in place. A pair of scissors is conveniently (ominously) placed near the robot. The onus, then, is on the viewer to either leave the robot to his own devices or cut the rope, providing the robot with what it so obviously wants. If cut, the robot moves towards the outlet until its hands make contact and, a few seconds later, a capacitor inside of the robot’s head blows up due to being overloaded. The only thing remaining being a cloud of smoke.

Made with: Mats Doornbos, Timothy Schuur
