Indoor Cats VS. Outdoor Cats: A Research Proposal

While research has been done on the communication possible between cats and humans, little investigation has been conducted on how this communication differs between cats that are allowed to go outside compared to those that are strictly indoor cats. This proposal reviews the current literature and proposes a research plan to fill this gap.

Analyzing the Impact of the Form of Navigational Guidance on Spatial Working Memory in Three-dimensional Environments

Using the popular videogame Minecraft as a research tool, we created multiple mazes for participants to collect three items in. For each maze, a different navigational method was provided to find the items (signs on the wall, written instructions, beacons of light). At the end, the participant was asked multiple questions about the maze to see how well they remembered their environment, which tested their spatial working memory. Results were mostly underwhelming due to a small sample size, subpar survey questions, and an imbalance in maze difficulty.
