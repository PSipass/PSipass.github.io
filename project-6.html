Human Critic VS. Machine Critic (Research Poster)

April 2025

Project Overview

Do people respond better to negative feedback from humans or machines? This is the question I, together with two classmates, tried to answer. We had participants solve a physical puzzle while being told a flat ‘no’ by either us or a digital voice whenever they made a mistake. Convincing the participants that we had an AI system sophisticated enough to recognize mistakes was the hard part. In truth, a GoPro aimed at the puzzle alongside a speaker we controlled from outside the testing environment functioned as the ‘machine’ critic. While statistically insignificant, six participants preferred the human critic, while only three preferred the ‘machine’ critic. Performance was not impacted but most participants did mention how the lack of any positive feedback from both critics was stressful. The most interesting finding was perhaps the reasoning behind the three participants’ preference for the ‘machine’ critic, as they mentioned that they did not feel as judged by the ‘machine’ critic compared to the human critic.
